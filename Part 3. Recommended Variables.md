
## **Additional Variables to Improve the Model**

To make the model more robust, it would be valuable to incorporate demographic, socioeconomic, and market-level variables that are not currently included.

### **Demographic and Socioeconomic Data (DANE)**

Public data from DANE (Departamento Administrativo Nacional de Estadística) can enrich customer profiles with:

* Age group
* Income level
* Socioeconomic stratum
* Household size
* Geographic location (urban/rural, region)

**Value:**
These variables help explain purchasing power and consumption patterns, which are key drivers of brand choice.

---

### **Market Research and Consumer Behavior (Nielsen)**

Data from Nielsen can provide insights such as:

* Market share by brand
* Category penetration
* Consumer preferences and trends
* Price sensitivity
* Promotion effectiveness

**Value:**
These variables add context to customer behavior, allowing the model to capture external market dynamics influencing purchase decisions.

---

### **Conclusion**

Incorporating demographic and market-level data would improve the model by combining who the customer is, how they behave, and what is happening in the market, leading to more accurate and actionable predictions.
